Economic Difficulties

Overview

The United States is the #1 superpower country, yet its economy is currently undergoing economic difficulties due to the pandemic. Few of the major reasons behind most of the United States's financial issues include taxes, healthcare costs, interest rates, and gender inequality. After the pandemic took over, Covid seems to be the #1 financial issue. Below is a chart and graph of the U.S. GDP that falls 3.5% in 2020 compared to 2019, which surprisingly is the biggest annual drop since 1946. Although there are other drops along the years seen on the graph, 2020's drop is noticeably lower than any other drop after 1946. In the article, data journalist Felix Richter states "According to the BEA, the GDP drop was caused by decreases in personal consumption expenditure (driven by a decrease in spending on services), exports and nonresidential fixed investments and partly offset by increases in federal government spending and residential fixed investment." The table above the graph displays the same information as the graph.
